Metric Geometry of Spaces and Groups

Course description: Metric geometry is the study of geometric properties such as curvature and dimensions in terms of distances, especially in contexts where the methods of calculus are unavailable, An important instance of this is the study of groups viewed as geometric objects, which constitutes the field of geometric group theory.
This course will introduce concepts, examples and basic results of Metric Geometry and Geometric Group theory. The course can be taken at the same time as or after a first course in Topology.

Syllabus:

  • Brief introduction to extrinsic and intrinsic curvatures and their relation to distances.
  • Metric spaces: Definition; various examples; some basic properties.
  • Hausdorff dimension.
  • Groups as geometric objects: Word metrics and Cayley graphs.
  • Coarse geometry and Quasi-isometries.
  • Basic hyperbolic geometry.
  • Gromov hyperbolicity and word hyperbolic groups.
  • Alexandrov spaces with curvature bounded below: some basic results.
