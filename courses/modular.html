INTRODUCTION TO MODULAR FORMS

Topics covered: The modular group and it's subgroups, Fundamental domain, Modular forms, Examples of Modular forms: Eisenstein series and cusp forms, Valence (dimension) formula, Petersson inner product, Hecke operators, L-functions: analytic continuation and functional equation.

Pre-requisites: Complex analysis

References:

  • A Course in Arithmetic, by J. P. Serre. Graduate Texts in Mathematics 7, Springer-Verlag, 1996.
  • Introduction to Elliptic curves and Modular forms, by Neal Koblitz. Graduate Texts in Mathematics 97, Springer-Verlag, 1984.
  • Topics in Classical Automorphic Forms, by Henryk Iwaniec. Graduate Studies in Mathematics 17, AMS 1997.
  • A First Course in Modular Forms, by Fred Diamond & Jerry Schurman. Graduate Texts in Mathematics 228, Springer-Verlag, 2005.
