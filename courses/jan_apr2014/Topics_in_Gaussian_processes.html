Topics in Gaussian processes

Prerequisites:

MA 261 (Probability models) or equivalent

MA 361 OR MA 222 or equivalent or either

This is a basic course covering several facets of Gaussian processes with emphasis on certain topics of current interest. In the first part of the course we shall cover many fundamental concepts such as continuity and boundedness of Gaussian processes, Isoperimetric inequality, Slepian's inequality, Gaussian Hilbert spaces etc. In the second part, we shall study recent results (and open problems) in zero sets or nodal lines of smooth Gaussian processes (in particular Gaussian analytic functions) in one and two dimensions.

 

Recommended books

  • Robert Adler and Jonathan Taylor, Gaussian Random Fields, Springer, New York, 2007.
  • Svante Janson, Gaussian Hilbert Spaces, Cambridge University Press, Cambridge, 1997).
  • V. I. Bogachev, Gaussian Measures, American Mathematical Society, Providence, RI, 1998.
