Topics in Complex Analysis

The focus of this "topics" course this semester will be dynamical systems in one complex dimension. The dynamical systems that we will study are those that arise from iterating a function that is holomorphic on the complex plane. The foundations of complex dynamics are best appreciated in the setting of compact spaces. For this reason, most of the course will be devoted to rational functions: these are the holomorphic self-maps of the Riemann sphere. Both the topological and the ergodic-theoretic aspects will be covered. The development of the latter will be self-contained.

 

Recommended books

  • J. Milnor, Dynamics in one Complex Variable, Annals of Mathematics Studies no. 160, Princeton University Press, 2006.
  • A.F. Beardon, Iteration of Rational Functions: Complex Analytic Dynamical Systems, Graduate Texts in Mathematics no. 132, Springer-Verlag 1991.
