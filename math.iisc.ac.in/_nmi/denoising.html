Development of Computationally Efficient Strategies for Fluorescence Image Denoising

Research Group

Phaneendra K. Yalavarthy and Sai Siva Gorthi

Supercomputer Education and Research Centre (SERC), IISc and Instrumentation & Applied Physics (IN&AP), IISc




Representative images of Red Blood Cells captured through micrscope, while they are flowing through the femtosecond laser micromachined microfluidic devices. Length of scale bar is 10μm. [IEEE JOURNAL OF SELECTED TOPICS IN QUANTUM ELECTRONICS, VOL. 21, NO. 4, JULY/AUGUST 2015. Article No: 6800106]


Image denoising has been a classic inverse problem and a routine image processing technique that is deployed to improve the image resolution/features. The fluorescence images of cellular and sub-cellular structures obtained in the microscope suffer from blur associated with the system response and aberrations. This blur is also spatially varying, denoising such images is not only challenging but very critical to obtain critical information. This project will develop efficient strategies, including compressive sensing based, for denoising such images and also evaluate them both numerically and experimentally for knowing their better utility.


Background needed:Linear Algebra (and/or) signal processing.


Basic Qualifications:B.E./B.Tech.in EE/ECE/IN/CS/IT/BME(or)M.Sc. (Mathematics/Physics)
