Mapping space in the brain

Research Group

Dr. Sachin Deshmukh and Dr. Rishikesh Narayanan

Center for Neuroscience, IISc and Molecular Biophysics Unit, IISc

Demonstration for a spatial map consisting of "place cells" in the hippocampus and graph-paper like "grid cells" in the medial entorhinal cortex (MEC) led to a Nobel Prize in 2014. This map can be used in navigation, like a GPS, as well as provide a spatial framework to store memories of what happened where, creating a "cognitive map". However, how this map is assembled remains a mystery. MEC is thought to create a spatial representation using path integration - a process of integrating velocity to estimate current displacement from the starting location. This process is inherently prone to accumulate errors as a consequence of integrating noisy velocity estimates. Recently, we showed that the lateral entorhinal cortex (LEC) encodes space derived from landmarks. The first aim of this project is to test the hypothesis that the landmark derived estimate of location from LEC is used to intermittently correct the "online" representation of path integration derived space from MEC. The second aim of this project is to test how information derived from a variety of sensory modalities is selectively incorporated in the spatial map. We will use state of the art wireless recording techniques to record activities of ensembles of neurons as well as local field potentials, quantitative analyses and modeling to understand how the hippocampus integrates LEC and MEC inputs to create a stable representation of space.
