Mathematical modeling of brain function

Research group

Sridharan Devarajan and Govindan Rangarajan

Centre For Neuroscience, IISc and Department of Mathematics, IISc

How do cognitive phenomena such as selective attention and decision-making emerge from neural processes in the brain? To address this question we follow a quantitative approach that combines mathematical modeling (linear/nonlinear dynamical systems, control theory, machine learning), large-scale computer simulations as well as neuroscience experiments.

To develop and refine our models we directly measure or perturb brain activity in human subjects performing cognitively demanding tasks. We employ a variety of cutting-edge techniques, including high-density electroencephalography (EEG), functional neuroimaging (fMRI), diffusion imaging (dMRI) and transcranial electrical and magnetic stimulation (tES/tMS).

We are looking for students interested in neuroscience, math and computing to join us. For a flavor of some of our work (past and ongoing), please visit: http://cns.iisc.ernet.in/sridharandevarajan/.

A few specific projects include:

  1. Applying machine learning and multivariate analysis techniques to identify the effect of attention on brain signals with fMRI.

  2. Developing and applying novel signal processing algorithms to characterize neural oscillations during attention in EEG.

  3. Designing Bayesian models of behaviour to understand the effects of brain stimulation on attention tasks.

  4. Building large-scale network models, based on a dynamical systems approach, with network connectivity constrained by diffusion MRI, to simulate brain activity.

For more details or other potential projects please email Dr. Sridharan Devarajan at sridhar@cns.iisc.ernet.in
